The autonomous follow-on encodes those already-approved verdicts and the adjudication note into the files, moving the contested figure into `verification.contested.positions[]` with its singular value withheld (D-14/VRFY-11). Most spike facts correctly remain `withheld-unverified` (matching SPIKE-01's finding that 0/3 met the strict historic bar); that is the correct, honest outcome, not a failure.

Purpose: The gate is only trustworthy if it operates on the project's own real records, not just fixtures. This turns the abstract machine into a demonstrated invariant over the spike corpus - and demonstrates BOTH claim standards on real data, or is honest about why one cannot yet be.
Output: Lineage tags on the registry, one worked verified authoritative fact, a worked corroborable fact or a recorded rationale, and one worked contested fact. This plan owns the src/_data edits exclusively.

Scope honesty (why autonomous false): authoring `verification.passes[]` verdicts and a `verification.adjudication.outcome` onto REAL published corpus facts is human editorial work, not an AI action. "AI may never adjudicate" (CLAUDE.md), "AI never writes a verdict; only a human edits it" (D-04), and VRFY-02 forbid an autonomous task from writing pass verdicts or adjudication outcomes into src/_data. This plan therefore mirrors the Phase 1 precedent exactly (01-10 Task 1, `checkpoint:human-action` gate="blocking", "Claude can assist with drafting and structuring, but the sourcing and verification are human editorial work"): a blocking human-action checkpoint authors/approves the pass verdicts and the adjudication note, and only the mechanical transcription of those human-approved values into the files stays autonomous. Note: this restriction applies to src/_data/** (real corpus). Synthetic test/fixtures authorship stays autonomous (handled in other plans).
